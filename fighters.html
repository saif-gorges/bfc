Roster

Pick Your Baby. Pick Your Poison.

★ Forever Fighting For The Tit-le ★

Eleven plastic warlords. Eleven different ways to ruin your night. Get to know them. Pick a favourite. Lock in your bet on the bet card before the bell rings.

01
Heir Apparent

Avery

a.k.a. "Daddy Rich"

Avery didn't earn it — she inherited the attitude and upgraded the damage. Raised on "yes," fueled by "mine," and never told no without consequences.

They say she once bought a bar just to throw someone out of it. They say her allowance has a body count. That pout? Weaponized. That tantrum? Contractually binding.

She doesn't fight fair — she fights offended. When the bell rings, Avery isn't here to win. She's here to make it your problem.

And Daddy always cleans up the mess.

02
5x Champion

Unibrow

"The Irish Problem"

Unibrow crawled out of a backroom somewhere in Dublin, smelling like bad decisions, dirty smoke and older whiskey than anyone could afford.

No one knows his real name — mostly because he's punched everyone who's tried to ask. That single eyebrow? Not genetics. That's generational rage stitched together over pints and poor choices.

Five-time champion — and somehow still fighting like he's got something to prove.

When the bell rings, it's not a match. It's just another story he won't remember… and you won't survive.

03
Sweet Until She Isn't

Latrice

"The Poundcake"

Latrice didn't earn the nickname — she proved it necessary. Sweet voice, soft smile… then the bell rings and it's like somebody dropped a brick in a bakery.

It's all in the name, baby. Poundcake ain't playing — she will pummel her opponents down and make bread with their tears. Latrice is a BEAST.

They say she came up fighting in places where apologies don't matter and pride gets folded into the floor. She doesn't talk much before a fight. No need.

Once she starts mixing it up, it's already too late to tell whether you're in a match… or in a recipe.

04
Self-Declared Warlord

Bruce Bree

Spliff-smoking. Slightly toasted.

This spliff-smoking warlord has commanded armies, ruined negotiations, and somehow still shows up late to his own victories — but it's never enough for Bruce Bree.

Half myth, half menace, always slightly toasted, he treats battle like a casual hobby and consequences like optional reading material.

Self-declared ultimate ruler, Bruce isn't here to compete — he's here to collect. He won't stop until the BFC trophy is sitting beside his throne, getting dusted off like another piece of loot from a long night of questionable decisions.

And if you're standing between him and it? That's on you, champ.

05
Glitter & Claws

Festival Kitty

a.k.a. "Baby Allie"

Sweet on the outside, glittery, smiling — looks like she wandered out of a festival and into the wrong story. But that's the trick.

Because Baby Allie is a burner babe with a soft voice and feral intentions. In the ring, the costume comes off and the claws come out — fast, messy, and way too confident for anyone's safety.

They call her Festival Kitty because she shows up like good vibes… and leaves like a warning.

Don't be fooled by the glitter. She bites.

06
He Doesn't Compete. He Torments.

Saw Baby

No name. Only whispers.

No one knows his real name — only the one left in whispers after it's already too late.

Shrouded in mystery, SAW BABY is only interested in inflicting pain on those around him. He couldn't care less about the title — he lives for blood, guts, gore, and the slow unraveling of whoever steps in front of him.

Cold, patient, and eerily calm, he treats every match like a puzzle box — each opponent just another piece to be broken, placed, and understood too late.

When the bell rings, it isn't the start of a fight. It's the moment you realize there is more on the line than the BFC title.

07
DIY Demolition

Tanya

"The Tool Babe"

Tanya isn't your average Scottish lass — she's what happens when DIY tutorials develop a drinking problem and a personality.

She can outdrink you, outbuild you, and out-argue your landlord all before lunch. If something's broken, she doesn't ask questions — she just sighs, takes a swig, and starts fixing it out of spite.

They call her The Tool Babe because she shows up with a toolbox, solves your life, and somehow leaves you feeling judged and improved at the same time.

Once Tanya picks up the nail gun, it's no longer a renovation — it's a warning.

08
Dress Code Optional

Bucky McNasty

Rural Canada's finest export.

Bucky McNasty — farm girl, nudist from rural Canada who grew up thinking "dress code" was just a suggestion city people made up for fun.

Mouth like a trucker and absolutely terrifying if you touch her fence line or her ego. She'll fix your tractor, forget your name mid-conversation, then beat you in a fight while asking if you've seen her missing boot.

They call her McNasty because "polite" never stood a chance. In the ring, she doesn't strategize — she just shows up, naked, confident, and swinging.

No fancy moves, no clean technique — just bare knuckles doing what they've always done best: taking the win.

09
Flow Through Impact

Hoopla

Free-spirited. Drum-circle dangerous.

Hoopla is a free-spirited hippy fighter who drifts in like music you can't quite turn off.

She spins a hula hoop like it's a weapon, a dance, and a life philosophy all at once — smiling through chaos, flowers in her hair, and absolutely zero interest in anyone's rules or timelines. Raised somewhere between a drum circle and a bad idea, she learned early that peace is optional — but rhythm is not.

She'll compliment your aura, offer you tea, then accidentally knock your lights out mid-spin and apologize sincerely while you're still falling.

When it's over, she just smiles, keeps spinning, and asks if anyone's tried breathing through it.

10
Loud. Wrong. Winning.

Ketamine Calf

Half-feral. Full-volume.

Half-feral, full-volume, and already treating life like it was a rave that forgot to end.

She parties like a natural disaster and fights like she's trying to keep the party going forever. Loud, unpredictable, and emotionally allergic to moderation. They say she once won a fight and immediately tried to turn it into an afterparty.

In the ring, she doesn't strategize — she just bounces in, causes confusion, and refuses to read the room.

And when it's over? She's already asking where the music went.

11
Bubblegum Trouble

Baby Ri Ri

Pink hair. Sass loaded.

Baby Ri Ri shows up like trouble dipped in bubblegum. Pink hair in space buns, sass loaded, and a smile that usually means someone's about to regret their decisions.

She talks fast, fights faster, and treats every confrontation like it's a personal inconvenience she plans to win anyway.

They call her Baby Ri Ri because she looks sweet enough to trust… right up until she isn't. In the ring, she doesn't strategize — she insults, interrupts, and improvises chaos with perfect timing.

When it's over? She fixes her space buns, tilts her head, and asks who thought that was a good idea.
